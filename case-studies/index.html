Our Professional Methodology

Comprehensive approach to economic analysis, damage calculations, and expert testimony. Our proven methodologies ensure accurate, defensible results for legal professionals.

Forensic Economics Methodology

1. Data Collection & Analysis

Comprehensive review of employment records, tax returns, financial statements, and economic data to establish baseline earning patterns.

2. Economic Modeling

Application of accepted economic principles and statistical methods to project future earnings, considering growth rates, inflation, and career progression.

3. Present Value Calculations

Discount future economic losses to present value using appropriate discount rates based on current economic conditions and risk factors.

4. Sensitivity Analysis

Testing various assumptions and scenarios to ensure robustness of conclusions and identify key variables affecting outcomes.

Business Valuation Methodology

1. Income Approach

Discounted cash flow analysis and capitalization of earnings methods to determine value based on expected future benefits.

2. Market Approach

Comparative analysis using market multiples from similar transactions and publicly traded companies in comparable industries.

3. Asset Approach

Assessment of tangible and intangible assets, considering book value adjustments and fair market values of underlying assets.

4. Reconciliation

Weighting of different approaches based on reliability of data, purpose of valuation, and nature of the business being valued.

Vocational Assessment Methodology

1. Individual Assessment

Comprehensive evaluation of education, work history, skills, and functional limitations through interviews and testing.

2. Labor Market Analysis

Research of local and regional employment opportunities, wage data, and job availability in relevant occupational categories.

3. Transferable Skills Analysis

Identification of skills that can be applied to alternative occupations, considering physical and cognitive requirements.

4. Earning Capacity Determination

Calculation of pre and post-incident earning capacity based on realistic employment alternatives and local wage data.

Life Care Planning Methodology

1. Medical Record Review

Comprehensive analysis of medical records, treatment history, and prognosis to understand current and future care needs.

2. Provider Consultation

Collaboration with treating physicians, therapists, and other healthcare providers to validate care recommendations.

3. Cost Research

Market research of medical costs, equipment prices, and service rates from multiple providers to ensure accurate pricing.

4. Present Value Analysis

Economic analysis of lifetime care costs, considering inflation rates, replacement schedules, and life expectancy factors.

Professional Standards & Quality Assurance

Peer Review Process

All analyses undergo internal peer review to ensure accuracy, completeness, and adherence to professional standards.

Documentation Standards

Comprehensive documentation of all assumptions, data sources, and methodologies to support transparency and replicability.

Continuing Education

Ongoing professional development to stay current with evolving economic methods, legal precedents, and industry standards.

Professional Ethics

Adherence to strict ethical guidelines ensuring objectivity, independence, and professional integrity in all engagements.
