Case Studies & Results

Real examples of our economic analysis expertise across diverse practice areas and case types. These anonymized case studies demonstrate our approach and the successful outcomes achieved for our attorney clients.

Personal Injury RI Superior Court

Construction Worker Catastrophic Injury

Challenge: 34-year-old union electrician suffered traumatic brain injury and spinal cord damage in construction accident, requiring lifetime care.

Our Approach: Comprehensive analysis including lost earning capacity over 30-year career, life care cost projections, and household services replacement value. Coordinated with life care planner and vocational rehabilitation expert.

Economic Analysis:

  • Pre-injury earnings trajectory: $95,000 annually with union progression
  • Present value of lost earnings: $2.1M
  • Life care costs: $3.2M over life expectancy
  • Household services: $450,000
Lost Earnings Life Care Planning Union Benefits
Medical Malpractice MA Superior Court

Birth Injury - Cerebral Palsy

Challenge: Failure to properly monitor fetal distress during delivery resulted in severe cerebral palsy, requiring lifetime medical care and attendant services.

Our Approach: Created comprehensive life care plan with present value economic analysis addressing medical expenses, equipment needs, and lost earning capacity due to disability.

Economic Analysis:

  • Future medical expenses: $2.8M present value
  • Attendant care services: $1.9M
  • Equipment and home modifications: $650,000
  • Lost earning capacity: $1.2M

Employment Litigation Federal District Court

Executive Wrongful Termination

Challenge: Senior technology executive wrongfully terminated in violation of employment contract. Complex compensation structure including base salary, bonuses, and equity compensation.

Our Approach: Analyzed back pay from termination date, front pay projections based on career trajectory, and quantified value of lost equity compensation and benefits.

Economic Analysis:

  • Back pay calculation: $385,000 (18 months)
  • Front pay projection: $1.2M over remaining career
  • Lost equity compensation: $450,000
  • Benefits and perquisites: $125,000
Executive Compensation Back Pay Equity Valuation
Shareholder Dispute RI Superior Court

Medical Practice Partnership Dissolution

Challenge: Departing physician partner disputed valuation methodology for buyout. Practice included multiple revenue streams and significant goodwill value.

Our Approach: Applied multiple valuation approaches including asset-based, income-based, and market-based methods. Addressed fair value vs. fair market value standards under state law.

Valuation Analysis:

  • Income approach (DCF): $2.8M practice value
  • Market approach: $2.6M based on comparable sales
  • Asset approach: $1.9M adjusted book value
  • Concluded fair value: $2.7M (no marketability discount)
Business Valuation Medical Practice Fair Value
FLSA Class Action Federal District Court

Restaurant Chain Overtime Violations

Challenge: Multi-location restaurant chain allegedly failed to pay proper overtime to assistant managers. Complex class action with 150+ affected employees.

Our Approach: Analyzed payroll records, scheduling data, and job descriptions to calculate unpaid overtime using DOL methodology. Statistical sampling for efficiency.

Damage Calculation:

  • Class size: 156 assistant managers
  • Time period: 3 years (statute of limitations)
  • Average unpaid overtime: $8,400 per class member
  • Total back wages: $1.31M
  • Liquidated damages: $1.31M
FLSA Class Action Overtime Calculations
Business Interruption MA Superior Court

Manufacturing Plant Fire Loss

Challenge: Fire damaged manufacturing facility requiring 8-month shutdown for rebuilding. Insurance dispute over business interruption coverage amount.

Our Approach: Calculated lost income during shutdown period, additional expenses incurred, and mitigation efforts. Analyzed pre-loss trends and seasonal variations.

Loss Analysis:

  • Pre-loss monthly revenue: $850,000 average
  • Shutdown period: 8 months
  • Lost gross income: $6.8M
  • Continuing expenses saved: $2.1M
  • Extra expenses incurred: $450,000
