Case Studies & Methodologies

Representative examples of our economic analysis expertise across diverse practice areas and case types. These anonymized case studies demonstrate our methodological approach to economic damage calculations and expert analysis.

Personal Injury RI Superior Court

Construction Worker Catastrophic Injury

Challenge: A 34-year-old union electrician suffered traumatic brain injury and spinal cord damage in a construction accident, requiring lifetime care.

Our Approach: Comprehensive analysis including lost earning capacity over 30-year career, life care cost projections, and household services replacement value. Coordinated with life care planner and vocational rehabilitation expert.

Economic Analysis Methodology:

  • Pre-injury earnings trajectory analysis with union progression patterns
  • Present value calculations of lost earnings capacity over working lifetime
  • Life care cost projections based on medical expert recommendations
  • Household services valuation using replacement cost methodology
Lost Earnings Life Care Planning Union Benefits
Medical Malpractice MA Superior Court

Birth Injury - Cerebral Palsy

Challenge: Failure to properly monitor fetal distress during delivery resulted in severe cerebral palsy, requiring lifetime medical care and attendant services.

Our Approach: Created comprehensive life care plan with present value economic analysis addressing medical expenses, equipment needs, and lost earning capacity due to disability.

Economic Analysis Methodology:

  • Future medical expense projections based on treatment protocols
  • Attendant care service cost calculations over life expectancy
  • Equipment and home modification cost analysis with replacement schedules
  • Lost earning capacity assessment considering disability impact
Life Care Planning Future Medical Costs Birth Injury
Employment Litigation Federal District Court

Executive Wrongful Termination

Challenge: A senior technology executive was wrongfully terminated in violation of their employment contract. The case involved a complex compensation structure including base salary, bonuses, and equity compensation.

Our Approach: Analyzed back pay from termination date, front pay projections based on career trajectory, and quantified value of lost equity compensation and benefits.

Economic Analysis Methodology:

  • Back pay calculation from termination through trial date
  • Front pay projection analysis based on career trajectory and industry standards
  • Equity compensation valuation using appropriate pricing models
  • Benefits and perquisites quantification including insurance and retirement contributions
Executive Compensation Back Pay Equity Valuation
Shareholder Dispute RI Superior Court

Medical Practice Partnership Dissolution

Challenge: A departing physician partner disputed the valuation methodology for their buyout. The practice included multiple revenue streams and significant goodwill value.

Our Approach: Applied multiple valuation approaches including asset-based, income-based, and market-based methods. Addressed fair value vs. fair market value standards under state law.

Valuation Analysis Methodology:

  • Income approach using discounted cash flow methodology
  • Market approach analysis based on comparable medical practice transactions
  • Asset approach assessment including adjusted book value calculations
  • Fair value determination considering applicable legal standards and marketability factors
Business Valuation Medical Practice Fair Value
FLSA Class Action Federal District Court

Restaurant Chain Overtime Violations

Challenge: A multi-location restaurant chain allegedly failed to pay proper overtime to assistant managers. This complex class action involved 150+ affected employees.

Our Approach: Analyzed payroll records, scheduling data, and job descriptions to calculate unpaid overtime using DOL methodology. Statistical sampling for efficiency.

Damage Calculation Methodology:

  • Class member identification and eligibility determination
  • Statistical sampling methodology for payroll record analysis
  • Overtime calculation using Department of Labor standards
  • Back wage calculations with appropriate interest and liquidated damages
FLSA Class Action Overtime Calculations
Business Interruption MA Superior Court

Manufacturing Plant Fire Loss

Challenge: A fire damaged a manufacturing facility, requiring an 8-month shutdown for rebuilding. An insurance dispute arose over the business interruption coverage amount.

Our Approach: Calculated lost income during shutdown period, additional expenses incurred, and mitigation efforts. Analyzed pre-loss trends and seasonal variations.

Loss Analysis Methodology:

  • Historical revenue analysis including seasonal and trend adjustments
  • Lost gross income calculation during shutdown period
  • Continuing expense analysis to determine saved costs
  • Extra expense documentation and quantification for mitigation efforts
