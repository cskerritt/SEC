When Is an Economic Expert Needed?

Personal injury attorneys in Rhode Island and throughout New England rely on economic experts in cases involving:

  • Catastrophic injuries with permanent disability or reduced work capacity
  • High-earning professionals where future income loss is substantial
  • Young victims requiring lifetime earnings projections
  • Self-employed individuals with complex income documentation
  • Wrongful death cases requiring household services and support calculations

Early involvement of a personal injury economist ensures comprehensive damage documentation and stronger settlement negotiations or trial presentations.

How We Calculate Damages

Lost Earnings & Earning Capacity

We calculate both past lost earnings from the time of injury and future earning capacity losses over the individual's remaining work life. Our analyses consider career progression, wage growth trends, and economic factors specific to the individual's industry and geographic region.

  • Historical earnings analysis using tax records and employment data
  • Future earning capacity projections based on career trajectory
  • Industry-specific wage growth analysis
  • Present value calculations using appropriate discount rates

Fringe Benefits & Employment Perquisites

Beyond base wages, we quantify the full value of employment benefits including health insurance, retirement contributions, paid time off, and other perquisites that would have been earned over the individual's career.

  • Health insurance and medical benefits valuation
  • Retirement and pension contribution losses
  • Paid vacation and sick leave calculations
  • Stock options and incentive compensation analysis

Household Services Replacement

For individuals unable to perform household tasks due to injury, we calculate the economic value of lost household services including childcare, home maintenance, and other domestic activities.

  • Childcare and family care services
  • Home maintenance and yard work
  • Household management and financial services
  • Transportation and errand services

Life Care Cost Analysis

For catastrophic injuries requiring ongoing care, we work with life care planners to quantify the present value of future medical expenses, equipment needs, and care services.

  • Medical treatment and therapy costs
  • Pharmaceutical and medical supply expenses
  • Durable medical equipment and assistive technology
  • Home modifications and accessibility improvements

Frequently Asked Questions About Personal Injury Economic Analysis

How quickly can a personal injury economist provide damage calculations?

We typically provide preliminary economic damage calculations within 3-5 business days of receiving basic case information. Full expert reports for personal injury cases generally require 2-3 weeks depending on complexity. Rush services are available for urgent cases.

What information does a personal injury economist need to calculate lost earnings?

To calculate economic damages, we need medical records detailing injuries and prognosis, employment records showing earnings history, tax returns for 3-5 years, education records, and work history. For vocational assessments, we also review functional capacity evaluations and transferable skills.

How do Rhode Island laws affect personal injury economic damage calculations?

Rhode Island personal injury cases follow specific statutes including RI Gen. Laws ยง 10-7-1 for wrongful death claims. Our economic analysis complies with these requirements, calculating pecuniary losses, economic support, and household services while properly accounting for personal consumption offsets as required by Rhode Island law.

Can you review opposing expert economic damage calculations?

Yes, we regularly provide rebuttal analyses of opposing economic experts' reports in personal injury cases. Our reviews identify methodological flaws, inappropriate assumptions, or calculation errors while clearly explaining why alternative approaches better reflect actual economic losses.

What makes a personal injury economist's testimony admissible in court?

Admissible economic expert testimony must meet court reliability standards using reliable methodologies, peer-reviewed data sources, and accepted calculation methods. Our personal injury economic analyses use Bureau of Labor Statistics data, actuarial worklife tables, and established present value calculations that consistently meet admissibility requirements in Rhode Island and federal courts.
