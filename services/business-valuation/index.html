What Is Business Valuation?

Business valuation is the process of determining the economic value of a business, professional practice, or ownership interest. As a certified valuation expert, I use recognized methodologies to establish defensible values for closely-held businesses, partnership interests, and professional practices. Business valuation provides the foundation for resolving disputes in litigation, divorce proceedings, shareholder conflicts, and estate matters throughout Rhode Island and New England.

With extensive experience in vocational rehabilitation and forensic economics, I bring a unique perspective as a valuation analyst, particularly in cases involving professional practices, service businesses, and companies where key-person issues affect value. My analyses address both economic value and the human capital factors that drive business performance, providing attorneys with comprehensive litigation support.

Business valuation expert services are essential in numerous legal contexts:

  • Divorce and Marital Dissolution: Determining the value of business interests for equitable distribution
  • Shareholder Disputes: Valuing minority interests in oppression and dissent cases

Rhode Island attorneys rely on my business valuation expertise to provide clarity in complex financial matters, ensuring fair outcomes for their clients.

How Our Valuations Support Your Case

As your business valuation expert, I employ the three standard approaches to business valuation, selecting the most appropriate methods based on the business type, available data, and litigation requirements. My valuation analyst methodology ensures defensible conclusions that support your case strategy.

Income Approach

  • Discounted Cash Flow (DCF): Projecting future cash flows and discounting to present value
  • Capitalized Earnings: Capitalizing normalized earnings for stable businesses
  • Excess Earnings Method: Appropriate for asset-intensive businesses

Market Approach

  • Guideline Company Method: Comparison to publicly traded companies
  • Guideline Transaction Method: Analysis of comparable business sales
  • Rules of Thumb: Industry-specific valuation multiples when appropriate

Asset Approach

  • Net Asset Value: Adjusted book value approach
  • Liquidation Value: For distressed or dissolving businesses
  • Replacement Cost: For asset-heavy operations

Frequently Asked Questions

What financial information does a business valuation expert need?

A business valuation expert typically needs 3-5 years of financial statements, tax returns, general ledger detail, accounts receivable/payable aging, fixed asset listings, and any existing buy-sell agreements. For professional practices, we also need patient/client lists, referral sources, and partnership agreements to complete a comprehensive business appraisal.

How does a valuation analyst determine the appropriate valuation date?

The valuation date depends on legal requirements and case purpose. In Rhode Island divorce cases, it may be the filing date, separation date, or trial date. For shareholder disputes, it's typically the triggering event date. Your business appraiser works with attorneys to select the appropriate date based on case facts and applicable law.

When do business valuation experts apply marketability or minority discounts?

Discount application depends on the standard of value. In fair market value contexts, marketability and minority discounts may apply. In Rhode Island fair value cases (like dissenting shareholder actions), discounts may be limited. A qualified business appraiser analyzes each case based on applicable law and professional standards.

How do valuation analysts distinguish personal from enterprise goodwill?

Business valuation experts analyze the practitioner's reputation, referral sources, non-compete agreements, and practice systems to distinguish personal (non-transferable) from enterprise (transferable) goodwill. This distinction is crucial in Rhode Island divorce cases where personal goodwill may not be marital property requiring division.

How quickly can attorneys get preliminary business valuations for litigation support?

Business appraisers can provide preliminary value ranges within 5-7 days to help Rhode Island attorneys assess case economics and settlement positions. Full valuation reports from a qualified business valuation expert typically require 3-4 weeks, depending on complexity and information availability.
