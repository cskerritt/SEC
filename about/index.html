Chris Skerritt, M.Ed, MBA, CRC, CLCP - Rhode Island Forensic Economist

Principal Economic Consultant

Experienced professional providing expert economic analysis and business valuation services to attorneys and businesses throughout New England. Court-tested professional expertise.

Professional Summary

Chris Skerritt, CRC, CLCP, is a leading forensic economist and expert witness serving Rhode Island and New England attorneys with comprehensive economic damage analysis. With over 15 years of court-tested experience, Chris combines advanced credentials in rehabilitation counseling, life care planning, and business administration to deliver thorough, defensible economic assessments that stand up to scrutiny in personal injury, medical malpractice, employment litigation, and commercial disputes.

Based in Smithfield, Rhode Island (400 Putnam Pike Ste J, Smithfield, RI 02917), Chris serves as a vocational counselor, economist, and consultant evaluator. His expertise spans forensic economics, labor market analysis, transferable skills evaluation, economic loss assessments, business valuation, and expert witness testimony across multiple jurisdictions.

Chris's multidisciplinary approach combines economic expertise with deep understanding of rehabilitation needs, making him uniquely qualified to assess the full financial impact of personal injury, medical malpractice, employment disputes, and commercial litigation. His testimony is known for being thorough, defensible, and accessible to non-economists.

Frequently Asked Questions

What qualifications does Chris Skerritt have as a forensic economist?

Chris Skerritt holds an MBA from Bryant University, is a Certified Rehabilitation Counselor (CRC), and a Certified Life Care Planner (CLCP). He has over 15 years of court-tested experience and has testified in Rhode Island, Massachusetts, Connecticut, and New Hampshire courts. His multidisciplinary credentials include specialized certifications in vocational evaluation, ergonomic assessment, and Medicare set-aside consulting.

What types of cases does Chris Skerritt handle as an expert witness?

As a forensic economist and expert witness, Chris Skerritt specializes in personal injury, wrongful death, medical malpractice, employment litigation, and commercial disputes. He provides comprehensive economic damage analysis, including lost earnings calculations, future medical cost projections, business valuation, and life care planning throughout Rhode Island and New England.

How does Chris Skerritt's approach differ from other forensic economists?

Chris Skerritt's unique multidisciplinary approach combines economic expertise with deep understanding of rehabilitation needs and vocational assessment. His dual credentials as both CRC and CLCP allow him to assess the full financial impact of injuries comprehensively. His testimony is known for being thorough, defensible, and accessible to judges and juries who may not have economic backgrounds.

In which jurisdictions has Chris Skerritt provided expert testimony?

Chris Skerritt has extensive court experience throughout New England, including Rhode Island Superior Court, Massachusetts Superior Court, Connecticut Superior Court, New Hampshire Superior Court, Workers' Compensation Courts, and multiple U.S. District Courts. His expertise is recognized across state and federal jurisdictions in the region.

What professional organizations is Chris Skerritt affiliated with?

Chris Skerritt serves as President Elect of the American Rehabilitation Economics Association (AREA) for 2024-2025. He is also an active member of the National Association of Forensic Economics (NAFE), American Academy of Economic and Financial Experts, National Association of Certified Valuators and Analysts (NACVA), and holds a Fellowship with the American Board of Vocational Experts (ABVE/F).
